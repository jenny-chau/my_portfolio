My interests lie in the intersection of technology, AI, and creating positive impact. With my background in biotech, I've gained an appreciation for projects that improve people’s lives, and I carry that motivation into software engineering. I enjoy building applications that are both practical and impactful — whether that’s creating tools that make everyday tasks easier or exploring how emerging technologies like AI can open new possibilities. What excites me most is using my skills to create something that genuinely helps others.

I’ll contribute to the company by combining my technical skills in full-stack development — using React, TypeScript, Python, and Flask — with the problem-solving and detail-oriented mindset I developed as a scientist. I’m proactive about learning and like to do my own research before asking for help, which allows me to get up to speed efficiently while respecting my teammates’ time. I thrive in collaborative environments, so when I do ask questions, they’re thoughtful and focused on moving projects forward. Most importantly, I’m motivated by building impactful applications and continuously learning as much as I can, so I’ll not only focus on delivering high-quality code, but also on creating solutions that make a real difference for users and align with the company’s goals.

I started my career in biotech, where I worked on developing antibody therapeutics. What I enjoyed most was tackling complex problems — designing experiments, analyzing data, researching potential solutions, and collaborating with others to progress projects. Over time, I realized I was just as fascinated by the tools and systems that made this work possible, and that curiosity led me to software engineering. I decided to make the switch because I wanted to apply the same critical thinking and problem-solving skills in a field where I could build scalable, user-focused applications and see the impact more directly. My goal is to work on projects that not only challenge me technically but also make a meaningful difference for others, just like the therapeutic work I contributed to as a scientist.

I thrive in a work environment that centers around passion, support, and transparency. I enjoy working with teammates who are excited about what they’re building, managers who encourage growth and collaboration, and a culture where open communication helps everyone do their best work.

Outside of work, I run a crochet small business and love making amigurumi plushies. With my software engineering skills, I’m working on building a website for my shop and exploring how technology can support the crochet community. For example, I’ve noticed challenges with digital product theft and resale, and I’m interested in how tools like blockchain and AI could help protect artists and customers. While these are ambitious goals, I enjoy learning and tackling complex problems.

I am based in the San Francisco Bay Area!
